Disc Over Water

Everson 1989

Experiment Design

Everson's experiment consisted of two rats – one control and one sleep deprived – who both had their sleep monitored by protruding electrodes. If the sleep deprived rat went to sleep, the disc beneath both rats would start moving. Unless the rats wake up and begin walking, they would subsequently be pushed off the platform into the water.

Despite these conditions for the sleep deprived rat, the control rat can sleep when the deprived rat is awake without the disc moving. Results

The longer the animals were awake, the more they ate. Remarkably, the more they ate while remaining awake, the more weight they lost. The point at which food intake began increasing was also the point when the rats ability to control body temperature failed.

This observation has led to the popular theory that sleep regulates body temperature, and as a result, metabolism. Ultimately the subjects in the experiment suffered a number of other detrimental side effects of sleep deprivation including fluid in the lungs, sores on skin, and brain and spinal cord death. Their final cause of death was septicemia, caused by immune system failure.
