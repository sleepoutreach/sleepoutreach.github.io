Catastrophes

Global and societal catastrophes caused by sleep deprivation

Global Catastrophes

Chernobyl Meltdown Disaster: April 1986

A routine shutdown and test of a reactor onsite was supposed to take place at 1 A.M. However, several safety features were mistakenly turned off by a sleep deprived set operators, resulting in reactor meltdown. It is estimated that the radiation from the Chernobyl meltdown disaster was 100 times more powerful than the atomic bombs dropped in World War II. 31 people died shortly after the explosion, but many thousands more have died from the long-term effects of radiation.

Exxon Valdez Disaster: March 1989

The Exxon Valdez oil tanker ran aground off Alaska after the captain turned over command to his first mate who was acutely sleep deprived. He had slept only 6 hours in the previous 3 days! The resulting damage on the environment was astonishing, with nearly half a million birds and five thousand sea otters dead (amongst other animals).

Societal Catastrophes

Vehicular Homicide: January 2006

In Union County, Florida an 18-wheel truck struck a Pontiac, rode over it, and proceeded to strike a school bus that was transporting 9 children home. All 3 vehicles proceeded to crash into a ditch, where 3 of the children were ejected from the bus. The accident ultimately resulted in the deaths of the school bus driver as well as all of the passengers in the Pontiac. The cause of the accident? Sleep deprivation. The truck driver had been awake for 34 hours straight, fallen asleep at the wheel, and caused the subsequent tragedy.
